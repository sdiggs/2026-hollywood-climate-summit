Why we're there

Meeting a new audience where it already gathers

The communications office generates a lot of news coverage every year, but news consumption is shifting and losing reach. This summit is a way to intersect with media and culture beyond the news cycle, and to find a new audience for storytelling about our science. The crowd is entertainment and creator-economy professionals across film and TV, music, gaming, and journalism, plus a corporate sustainability track focused on greening the industry itself.

Many of these folks have never met a working climate scientist. The goal is simple: be approachable, be human, and leave people curious enough to want to visit campus. We're just a couple of hours down the road from Hollywoodmakes us local, which is a real advantage for us. Let's lean into that.

Your pass and the app

Getting your credentials

Check your spam

You'll get two emails from Hollywood Climate Summit 2026 <team@agorify.com>, and they'll almost certainly land in your spam or junk folder. Go look for them now so you're not scrambling on the morning of.

  1. Create your Agorify accountThe first email prompts you to set up an account at agorify.com. That's the summit's event app, with venue info, networking, and schedules.
  2. Find your passThe second email holds your pass and ticket as a QR code, which is how you get into the venues each day.
  3. Install the appYou can pull up the same QR ticket in the Agorify app under the top-left menu, then Event Ticket. Install it on your phone, at least for the days of the summit.

At the table

What to talk about

This audience is removed from real climate scientists and may carry assumptions about who we are, where we come from, and what we look like. You get to gently rewrite that. A few openers that tend to land:

  • What drew you to science in the first place
  • What you study, and why that subject grabbed you
  • How your work intersects with climate change, and what climate change means for your field, whether that's diminishing ecosystems, an organism under stress, melting glaciers, or rising temperatures
  • What a day in your life actually looks like, from fieldwork to the lab to the screen
  • Your genuine passion for the work
  • How climate research and environmental storytelling could show up better in entertainment media

If someone wants to learn more about Scripps or visit campus, connect them with Robert Monroe or Lauren Fimbres Wood.

Dress code

What to wear

Last year the suggestion was business casual with a sustainable mindset: rewear what you already own, borrow, or lean on vintage and sustainable brands. It was not a fancy crowd, so comfortable and put-together is the right read. Reference photos of what our group wore last year are in the shared drive folder.
